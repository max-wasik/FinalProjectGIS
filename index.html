Top 10 Sights in Graz

An interactive StoryMap

Created by Maximilian, Patrick, Emilia and Lena

Welcome to our interactive StoryMap of Graz! This project guides you through the city’s ten most iconic attractions which are places that reflect the rich cultural heritage, architectural diversity and vibrant urban life of Austria’s second-largest city. From Renaissance buildings in the historic Old Town and baroque palaces to modern art museums and lively markets, Graz offers a unique blend of history and contemporary culture.

As you scroll, the map will automatically follow your journey, zooming in on each landmark to give you a deeper geographic sense of how the city is structured. Whether you're planning a visit, exploring for academic purposes, or simply discovering new corners of Graz, this StoryMap provides a visual introduction to the most important sites that shape the character of the city.

1. Schloßberg

The Schloßberg is the landmark of Graz, which features the famous clock tower, a unique view over the city, and rich historical significance.

2. Old Town of Graz

The Old Town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and offers narrow alleys, picturesque squares, and diverse architecture.

3. Eggenberg Palace

A baroque palace surrounded by gardens, rich in history and art.

4. Styrian Armoury

The Armoury houses the largest historical weapons collection in the world.

5. Graz Clock Tower

The most famous symbol of Graz, located on the Schloßberg.

6. Kunsthaus Graz

The futuristic “Friendly Alien” is one of Austria’s most modern museums.

7. Kaiser-Josef-Platz Market

Here you can find regional products from Styria in a traditional market atmosphere.

8. Hauptplatz (Main Square)

The central square of Graz, which is surrounded by historic buildings and cafés.

9. Double Spiral Staircase

An architectural highlight from the Renaissance, consisting of two interwoven staircases.

10. Kastner & Öhler – Paradeishof

A historic department store with a modern interior and a popular rooftop bar.
